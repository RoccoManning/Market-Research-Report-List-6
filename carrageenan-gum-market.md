<p><ul><li>Iota</li><li>Kappa</li><li>Lambda</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Carrageenan gum is classified into three main types based on their molecular structures and properties: Iota, Kappa, and Lambda. Iota carrageenan forms soft and elastic gels, making it ideal for use in dairy products and desserts. Kappa carrageenan creates firm and brittle gels and is commonly used in meat and dairy products. Lambda carrageenan does not gel but is used as a thickening agent in dairy and beverage applications. Each type of carrageenan gum serves specific functions in food and beverage production.</p></p>
