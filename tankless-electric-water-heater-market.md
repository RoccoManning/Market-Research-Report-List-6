<p><p>Tankless electric water heaters come in two main market types: whole-house and point-of-use. Whole-house tankless electric water heaters are designed to provide hot water for the entire house, allowing for simultaneous use of multiple hot water fixtures. On the other hand, point-of-use tankless electric water heaters are installed near specific hot water fixtures, such as sinks or showers, to provide instant hot water without the need for a traditional water tank. Both types offer energy efficiency and space-saving benefits.</p></p>
