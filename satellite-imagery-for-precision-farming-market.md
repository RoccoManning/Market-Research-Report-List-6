<p><p>Satellite Imagery for Precision Farming consists of two main types: Optical Imagery and Radar Imagery. Optical Imagery involves capturing images using visible and infrared light, allowing farmers to monitor crop health, growth patterns, and identify pests or diseases. On the other hand, Radar Imagery uses radar waves to penetrate through clouds and vegetation, providing valuable information on soil moisture levels, crop structure, and yield predictions. Both types of imagery play a crucial role in helping farmers make informed decisions to optimize their farming practices.</p></p>
