<p><p>Railroad weighing scales can be categorized into two main types: static and dynamic. Static scales are stationary and require the train to come to a complete stop for accurate weighing. On the other hand, dynamic scales can measure the weight of the train while it is moving at a slow speed, eliminating the need for the train to stop. Both types of scales have their own advantages and are used based on the specific requirements of the railroad operation.</p></p>
