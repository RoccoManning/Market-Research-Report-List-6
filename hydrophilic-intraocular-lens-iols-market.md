<p><p>The hydrophilic intraocular lens (IOL) market is divided into two main types: non-pre-loaded IOLs and pre-loaded IOLs. Non-pre-loaded IOLs require manual loading into the injector before implantation, offering flexibility for surgeons but potentially increasing the risk of handling errors. In contrast, pre-loaded IOLs come pre-assembled in a sterilized injector, streamlining the surgical process and enhancing efficiency while reducing the risk of contamination and errors during loading. Both types cater to varying surgical preferences and patient needs.</p></p>
