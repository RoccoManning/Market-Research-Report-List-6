<p><p>Injection peptide drugs are administered in two main ways: intravenous injection and subcutaneous injection. </p><p>Intravenous injection involves delivering the drug directly into the bloodstream, allowing for rapid absorption and immediate effect. This method is commonly used for drugs that require quick and precise dosing, such as emergency treatments or chemotherapy.</p><p>Subcutaneous injection involves delivering the drug into the layer of fat tissue just beneath the skin. This method allows for slower absorption and prolonged effect, making it suitable for drugs that require sustained release, such as insulin or growth hormone treatments.</p></p>
