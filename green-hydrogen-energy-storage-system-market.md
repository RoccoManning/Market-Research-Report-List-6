<p><p>Green hydrogen energy storage systems come in three main types based on the state of the hydrogen: gas, liquid, and solid. Gas storage involves compressing the hydrogen to store it in tanks or pipelines. Liquid storage involves cooling the hydrogen to very low temperatures to turn it into a liquid form for easier storage. Solid storage involves using materials such as metal hydrides to absorb and release hydrogen gas. Each type of storage has its own advantages and applications in various industries.</p></p>
