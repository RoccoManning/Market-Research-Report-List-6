<p><p>Microfluidic flow control systems are categorized into different types namely, chips, pumps, syringes, sensors, connectors, and others. Chips refer to the actual microfluidic devices where fluid flow is controlled. Pumps are used to regulate the flow rate of the fluid. Syringes are used for injecting or withdrawing fluids. Sensors are used to monitor various parameters of the fluid flow. Connectors are used to link different components of the system. Other types may include valves, controllers, and mixers among others.</p></p>
