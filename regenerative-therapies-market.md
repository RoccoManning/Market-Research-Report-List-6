<p><p>Regenerative therapies market includes allografts, xenografts, and alloplasts. Allografts involve using tissue or cells from a donor of the same species, often sourced from cadavers. Xenografts use tissue or cells from a different species, such as pigs or cows. Alloplasts are synthetic materials made in a lab, like ceramics or polymers. Each type offers different benefits and limitations for regenerative medicine applications, providing options for patients and clinicians seeking regenerative treatments.</p></p>
