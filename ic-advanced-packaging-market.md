<p><p>IC Advanced Packaging refers to the methods used to package integrated circuits for improved performance and efficiency. Two common types are 3D and 2.5D packaging. In 3D packaging, multiple layers of integrated circuits are stacked on top of each other, allowing for higher levels of integration and reduced signal lengths. 2.5D packaging involves placing a separate silicon interposer between the die and substrate, enabling improved thermal performance and higher bandwidth connectivity. These advanced packaging methods are crucial for meeting the increasing demands of modern electronic devices.</p></p>
