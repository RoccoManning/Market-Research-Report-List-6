<p><p>Quantitative respirator fit testing is a method used to assess the effectiveness of a respirator by measuring the leakage of potentially harmful airborne substances into the mask. The PortaCount test is a computerized system that provides real-time measurements of particulate leakage. The CNC test uses a controlled negative pressure to detect leaks in the respirator seal. The CNP test, on the other hand, utilizes pressurized aerosol to detect any leakage. Each of these methods offers unique advantages in accurately assessing the fit of a respirator.</p></p>
