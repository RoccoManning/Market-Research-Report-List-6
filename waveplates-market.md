<p><ul><li>Quarter Waveplates (QWP)</li><li>Half Waveplates (HWP)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Waveplates are optical devices that alter the polarization state of light. Quarter waveplates (QWPs) and Half waveplates (HWPs) are two common types in the market. QWPs introduce a phase difference of 90 degrees between two orthogonal polarization components, while HWPs introduce a phase difference of 180 degrees. QWPs are often used for circular polarization conversion and interference reduction, while HWPs are used for polarization control and compensation. Both types have various applications in industries such as telecommunications, medical imaging, and spectroscopy.</p></p>
