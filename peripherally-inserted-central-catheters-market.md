<p><p>Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICCs) are commonly used in the medical field for long-term intravenous access. There are two main types of PICCs on the market: Power-Injected Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters and Conventional Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters. The difference between the two lies in their ability to withstand higher pressure during contrast-enhanced imaging procedures. Power-Injected PICCs are designed to handle these higher pressures, making them suitable for patients who require frequent imaging studies, while Conventional PICCs are more commonly used for standard intravenous therapy.</p></p>
