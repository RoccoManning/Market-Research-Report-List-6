<p><p>Cardiac biomarkers are used to diagnose and monitor heart conditions. Troponin, CK-MB, and myoglobin are protein biomarkers that indicate damage to the heart muscle. Natriuretic peptides - BNP and NT-proBNP, are hormone biomarkers that reflect heart failure. Other biomarkers include CRP and D-dimer, which indicate inflammation and blood clotting. Each biomarker plays a specific role in diagnosing and managing heart disease, providing valuable information to healthcare providers for accurate treatment decisions.</p></p>
